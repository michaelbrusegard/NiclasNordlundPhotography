Administrative Details

Our client is Niclas Nordlund, a photographer from Åland, Finland. He is a freelance photographer who specializes in photos of animals, landscapes, events and more, and has been looking for an easier way for customers to reach him, as well as a way to showcase his previous work.


Testing Document

A brief summary of the questionnaire you send out to testers, as well as the link to the Google Form you used. Don't forget to ask your client to test the website.

The questionnaire contained a link to the website and a brief introduction to our project. It started out with questions about device, age and amount of bugs found, for sorting the data. The rest of the questions were about the website itself, and how the user felt about it. The questionnaire can be found here. We then asked about user experience, and how the user felt about the website. In the end, we asked if anything did not work as expected, and if the user had any other comments.


Tester Audience

A description of your efforts to reach testers that are similar to the website's intended audience and how successful you were (give the percentages of your tester audience once you aggregate their answers, e.g., 23% of college students, 46% school administrators, etc.) If you are collecting age-ranges, you can do the same. The target audience for our client is mainly companies, and seeing how it's not as simple for us to reach out to the preferred target audience, we had friends and family test the website for us. We have recieved answers from ages ranging 21 years to 79 years with the average age of our testers combined being 43. The answer diagram is shown below. We also contacted our client, Niclas, and asked him to provide feedback in the same way as in the questionnare.


Testing Platforms

The testers were asked which devices they used to test the website, and the results are as follows: 70.6% on phone, 29.4% on computer. Respondents were asked about their browser, and the results are as follows: 52.9% on Safari, 35.3% on Chrome, and 11.8% on Firefox. The answer diagrams are shown below.


Feedback Summary

A summary of the feedback you received organized by question type (design, navigation, content, usability, etc.). Highlight both positive comments and thoughtful criticism, your client's testimonial, etc.

A lot of the comments we recieved from those testing were that the first impression of the website was good, in general people were fond of the design choices we have made for our showcase sites. When it comes to user friendliness a lot of the feedback was positive regarding navigating through the website, however we got some valuable comments on a few elements of the site which could be improved. For instance when you are in the checkout menu it is not intuitive how to remove the selected images from your shopping cart, and some users reported problems with the carousel and text on the home page. Our Response

All suggestions from the testers are feasible for us to implement, and we have already made some changes to the website based on the feedback we recieved. We fixed the "back to top" button to the bottom of the home page, which was not working during testing. Another issue we decided to fix were the problems that occured with the carousel and the text for the home page. We have not started to work on a more intuitive checkout menu, but we have ideas that would involve having a "cross-out" button in the top right of each shop item that would remove the item from the cart.


Self-reflection

Teamwork and communication is key. We have learned how difficult it can be to work with several people in the same documents and quite a bit about Git. One of the group members spent a couple of weeks without being able to fetch the Git repo, but luckily the team was able to help him out. We have also learned how difficult it can be to communicate with a client. Neither party had any experience with making a website, so it wasn't easy to know what could and couldn't be done.
